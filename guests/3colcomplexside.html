#leftbox

  • » Absolute position
  • » Fixed width
  • » Width easily changed

Known Problems

  • » If the height of the content in #leftbox exceeds the height of the content in #container, the background color will fill the browser width space under that section.
  • » Watch out for long, unbreakable text or unwrappable links as they will spill out of the fixed width sidebar.

#rightbox

  • » Absolute position
  • » Fixed width
  • » Background is an image

Known Problems

  • » Content in this sidebar that exceeds the height of the middle section will spill out of the div and cause the background color of the page to fill the newly available space.
  • » Watch out for content that doesn't wrap, such as long links, words, or even images that are too wide.
  • » To change the width of this div, the background image must be made wider (or narrower). The image used here has a narrow border on the left side that is part of the image.
  • » Be aware of IE5-Mac and its secret margin on right positioned elements.

Hello! Welcome to my pad!

I hope you enjoy your time here. Test things out, change text sizes, change the browser width, get a feel for how everything works. Below is a little information about what you'll find here.

At the top of the page, the #header has a background image (and I'm rather fond of it, too). The links below it are an unordered list that will wrap nicely if needed.

To your left and right, you will find absolutely positioned sidebars. Each uses a different technique for its background color. There is a little summary in each sidebar about its attributes.

All of the content on this page except the #leftbox and the #header falls within a div called #container. This pad, er, box I'm in is here mostly so I have somewhere to sit and talk. Below you will find the rest of the layout, two floated columns and another div that all fit nicely between the sidebars.

Known Problems

  • »  #wrapper needs a width for IE5.5 but cannot have one for IE6 so a hack must be employed.
  • » IE doubles margins of floats on the side they contact their container, so different values must be provided for other browsers for both the left and right floats.
  • »  IE5/Mac has problems if there are margins or padding on the clearing div.

Good Things

  • »  Since these divs are both floated, there will be no 3px text-jog in IE if one div is longer than the other.
  • » Flies, flies, and more flies! Oh, and my lily pad as well.
  • »  Oops! How did that last line get in here?
 

#contents

  • » In theory this is a static box following the clearing div
  • » In practice it is positioned relatively so IE6 will display it
  • » It can fill the entire width between sidebars if desired

The footer for this page is currently within the #container div. Although this layout doesn't have one, a full width footer can be added. Just put another div after the close of the #container div and style it however you choose!

You'll find the technical details of this layout on the previous page. All the CSS is in the head of each document, where you will hopefully find some helpful comments as well.

So folks, that's about all. I hope you have enjoyed your visit and that the information and techniques you found here are useful to you.
